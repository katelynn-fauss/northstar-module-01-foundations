# Customer Count Decline Analysis — Brewbird Coffee Roasters

A structured worksheet for diagnosing what's driving customer count decline at the flagship Seattle location, and building a recommendation on whether to close or reposition it.

---

## Purpose

This worksheet guides the analysis from question to recommendation. It follows a phased structure — Define, Collect, Hypothesize, Analyze, Recommend — ensuring the work stays scoped to the decision at hand rather than drifting into general exploration.

**The decision this informs:** Whether to close or reposition the Seattle flagship location.

**What done looks like:** A recommendation with supporting evidence — a clearly identified root cause (or ruled-out causes) tied to a specific close/reposition conclusion, backed by data.

---

## Phases

### 1. Define
Lock in the question, the decision it informs, and what a complete answer looks like. Don't move forward until these are agreed on with stakeholders.

### 2. Collect
Inventory the data needed to answer the question. Separate what exists from what needs to be acquired. Note gaps that could block or weaken the analysis.

**Current data inventory:**
| Dataset | Status | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Foot traffic / visit counts | ✅ Available | 6 months |
| POS / transaction history | ❌ Not accessible | — |
| Customer cohort / loyalty data | ❌ Not accessible | — |
| Competitive & local context | ❌ Not accessible | — |
| Customer reviews (Google, Yelp) | ⚠️ To be pulled | — |

**Known gaps to address:**
- 6 months of traffic data may not capture the full decline curve or seasonal patterns — check whether older records exist.
- Transaction data is needed to distinguish fewer visits from lower spend per visit. This distinction materially changes the close vs. reposition recommendation.
- Review trends (Google/Yelp) are a quick, free source of qualitative signal and should be pulled early.

### 3. Hypothesize
*(Forthcoming)* — Generate candidate explanations for the decline before looking at the data. This prevents the analysis from anchoring on whatever pattern appears first.

### 4. Analyze
*(Forthcoming)* — Test each hypothesis against available data. Document what each dataset confirms, weakens, or rules out.

### 5. Recommend
*(Forthcoming)* — Synthesize findings into a close/reposition recommendation with supporting evidence and confidence level.

---

## Key Scoping Decisions

- **Metric:** Customer count is the primary metric. Revenue per customer and visit frequency should be tracked alongside it — a count decline with flat revenue tells a different story than both falling together.
- **Comparison baseline:** A comparable location or this location's own historical trend is needed to distinguish a local problem from a broader one.
- **Stakeholder:** Identify the decision-maker and their prior lean before finalizing the recommendation format.
