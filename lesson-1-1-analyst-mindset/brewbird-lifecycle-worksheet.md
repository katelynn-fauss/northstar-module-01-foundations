# Brewbird Lifecycle Worksheet

**Analyst:** [Katelynn Fauss]
**Date:** [06/08/2026]
**Client:** Brewbird Coffee Roasters
**Stakeholder:** Carmen Ruiz-Santana, CEO

## 1. Define

The question: What is driving the customer count decline at the flagship Seattle location?

The decision this will inform: Whether to close or reposition the location

What "done" looks like: A recommendation with supporting evidence — a clearly identified root cause (or ruled-out causes) tied to a specific close/reposition conclusion, backed by data

## 2. Collect

Datasets needed:
- Foot traffic / visit counts (Seattle flagship + at least one comparison location)
- POS / transaction history (to separate count decline from spend decline)
- Customer cohort or loyalty data (to distinguish lost regulars vs. fewer new visitors)
- External context: nearby competitor openings, construction, transit changes, population shifts
- Customer exit surveys or review trends (Yelp, Google) for qualitative signal

Datasets that already exist:
- Foot traffic / visit counts — 6 months of history

Datasets that don't exist or aren't accessible:
- POS / transaction history
- Customer cohort / loyalty data
- Competitive & local context data
- Qualitative feedback (surveys, reviews)
